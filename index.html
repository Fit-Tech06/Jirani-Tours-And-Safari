Tanzania's Iconic National Parks

Explore Africa's Wildlife Sanctuaries

Serengeti National Park

Home to the Great Wildebeest Migration, the Serengeti offers unparalleled wildlife viewing with over 1.5 million wildebeest, zebras, and gazelles. Witness the Big Five and experience one of the world's most spectacular natural events.

Kilimanjaro National Park

Africa's highest peak at 5,895 meters. Climb through diverse ecosystems from rainforest to arctic summit. Experience breathtaking views and the achievement of conquering one of the world's Seven Summits.

Ngorongoro Crater

A UNESCO World Heritage Site and the world's largest inactive volcanic caldera. This natural amphitheater hosts incredible wildlife density, including the Big Five, in a stunning 260 km¬≤ crater floor.

Mikumi National Park

Tanzania's fourth-largest park, offering budget-friendly safari experiences. Spot elephants, lions, giraffes, and diverse birdlife in the Mkata floodplain, often called "Little Serengeti".

Tarangire National Park

Famous for its massive elephant herds and ancient baobab trees. During dry season, witness thousands of animals gathering around the Tarangire River, creating spectacular wildlife concentrations.

Lake Manyara National Park

A diverse park featuring the alkaline Lake Manyara, dense forests, and open grasslands. Known for tree-climbing lions, large elephant herds, and over 400 bird species including thousands of flamingos.

Mkomazi National Park

Home to rare species including the endangered African wild dog and black rhino. This remote park offers stunning scenery, diverse ecosystems, and exceptional opportunities for wildlife photography.

Saadani National Park

Tanzania's only park bordering the Indian Ocean, combining wildlife and beach experiences. Enjoy game drives followed by relaxing on pristine beaches, creating a unique safari-beach combination.

Arusha National Park

Diverse ecosystems with Mount Meru views, Ngurdoto Crater, and Momela Lakes. Perfect for day trips, offering walking safaris, canoeing, and excellent bird watching in a compact, accessible setting.

The Great Wildebeest Migration

One of Nature's Greatest Spectacles

The Serengeti Wildebeest Migration is one of the most awe-inspiring wildlife spectacles on Earth. Each year, over 1.5 million wildebeest, along with hundreds of thousands of zebras and other grazers, embark on a dramatic journey across the Serengeti National Park in Tanzania and the Maasai Mara in Kenya.

Key Highlights

  • Annual Event: The migration occurs year-round, with different groups moving at different times, creating a continuous flow of wildlife.
  • Spectacular Crossings: Famous for dramatic river crossings, where herds brave crocodile-infested waters to reach lush new grazing grounds.
  • Ecological Significance: Supports the health of the ecosystem by maintaining the balance between predator and prey, and influences the landscape.
  • Wildlife Viewing: Offers unparalleled opportunities for wildlife photography and safaris, with predators like lions and cheetahs capitalizing on the migrating herds.

Migration Cycle

December - March
Gather in Southern Serengeti and Ndutu area; over 500,000 calves are born
April - May
Move northwest through the Serengeti in massive columns
June - July
Reach the Western Corridor and Grumeti River with dramatic crocodile crossings
August - October
Cross into Kenya's Masai Mara, facing treacherous Mara River crossings
November
Begin journey back south as rains begin
